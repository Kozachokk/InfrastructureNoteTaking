What is BIOS?

BIOS is actually UEFI in modern computers and it's firmware that makes your pc run. Its core features are:

  • Power-On Self-Test(POST): Check every hardware component to make sure everything works well
  • Bootstrap Loader: Loads the bootloader of your operating system and runs it
  • CMOS Setup Utility: It's an interface program(GUI) that is the settings app for your hardware
  • Interface with Hardware: It's the link between your operating system and the hardware

BIOS is firmware == code, so that means it can be updated and that's where flashing the BIOS comes in. People generally do it to update the old BIOS to support new technologies.

The BIOS is a chip on your motherboard which is the central part of your pc, connecting everything together and it gives power to your CPU and RAM.
The motherboard also has ports on which you can connect all of your devices such as mouse, keyboard, screen, speakers, etc.
